Exercises by Muscle Groups

Chest and Back

Bench Press

The barbell bench press is a traditional exercise that is well-liked throughout all weight lifting communities. The bench press is a fundamental chest exercise in almost every fitness regimen, from bodybuilders to powerlifters. The pectoralis major is the main muscle worked by bench pressing (your chest).

Chest Fly

Cables are unbeatable when it comes to fly variants. They enable constant tension throughout the entire range of motion of the activity. After your presses, perform your flyes as either your first isolation exercise or your last activity of the workout.

Lat Pulldown

It's terrific for growing muscle since it gives the lats a wider range of motion and more time under tension. On these, slow down the pace of each rep, squeeze firmly at the bottom, and allow a good stretch at the top.

Seated Lat Row

The traditional seated row, in contrast to every free-weight version here, keeps tension constant throughout the whole exercise. Additionally, many gyms feature a variety of handles that you may use to create a variety of wide and narrow grips.

Biceps and Triceps

Dips

Dips are a well-known standalone mass-builder. Dips work the triceps more than the chest, so maintain as much upright posture as you can. Maintain a straight back and keep your elbows tucked down close to your sides.

Barbell Curl

The short and long heads of the biceps are similarly worked out by the typical shoulder-width curl. When you can really push yourself with weight, start your biceps workout with heavy curls. Select a weight that will allow you to complete 6-8 repetitions for a little more of a strength stimulus.

Hammer Curl

Curls performed with a palms-facing or neutral grip substantially engage the brachialis in addition to the biceps. By "raising" your biceps from underneath, a larger brachialis can make your entire arm bigger.

Cable Push-Down

This exercise is widely regarded as the classic triceps burnout technique for good reason. It is simple to do, effectively targets the lateral head, and lends itself well to dropsets.

Legs and Delts

Squats

Because they are the most difficult leg exercise you can perform, squats are king. They engage every muscle in the lower body and have been found to increase the production of the hormones that help build muscle. In fact, it has been demonstrated that squatting before practicing curls dramatically increases arm strength!

Leg Press

The leg press enables a range of foot positions to stimulate the hamstrings, glutes, outer quads, or inner quads. Take cautious not to lower the sled too much, though! 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions are a safe bet.

Lateral Raise

Although it's one of the best and most well-liked isolation exercises in the gym, this exercise is trickier to master than it first appears. The two cues to lead with your elbows and to only proceed down until your arms are around 30 degrees out from your sides are usually helpful for beginners.

Rear Deltoid Machine

Your posterior deltoids are developed using the rear delt fly machine, which develops your shoulder muscles. The rear delt fly machine offers far more stability than free weight exercises like the bent over reverse fly.
